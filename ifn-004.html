IFN-004

$28,000 Over 3 Years

The Slow-Burn Pattern · Below Detection Threshold

John Dee · OSB
2025
Match.com
Maximum Financial Duration · I Score 5/5

Low velocity. High duration. Maximum damage. $28,000 extracted across 36 months — averaging $778 per month — deliberately designed to remain below detection thresholds while compounding total loss.

The Pattern

The scammer did not ask for a large sum. They never did. Every request was manageable — the kind of amount that feels like helping, not losing. $500 here. $800 there. Sometimes less. Over 36 months on Match.com, this pattern extracted $28,000 from a single victim without triggering a single fraud alert.

The Architecture

This is deliberate design. The slow-burn pattern is not a failed big ask — it is a successful small-ask strategy. The scammer understood that large requests trigger scrutiny. Small requests, framed within a relationship of trust, pass without question. Repeated monthly. For three years.

The Mathematics

$28,000 ÷ 36 months = $778 average monthly extraction. That figure is calibrated. It sits below most bank alert thresholds. It is small enough to feel like a normal relationship expense — a gift, a loan, an emergency. It is large enough, compounded over time, to constitute serious financial crime.

The Detection Problem

Standard fraud detection failed this case entirely. No single transaction was large enough to flag. The relationship context neutralised suspicion at every stage. Only a behavioural framework — one that scores relationship duration, emotional investment, and extraction pattern together — would catch this. That framework is the RSEI Model.

Methodology Note
IFN-004 holds the highest combined RSEI score in the current case library — 18/20. The slow-burn pattern is classified as the highest-sophistication financial extraction technique in OSB's documentation, precisely because it is the least visible and the most damaging over time.
