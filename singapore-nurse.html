From Love to Loss:
A Singapore Nurse's Romance Scam Story

John Dee · OSB
August 2025
2.2K views · 86 upvotes
Scam Information and help

🚨 Real story. Please read and learn — it can happen to anyone.

⚠️ If you're going to judge the victim, please don't read this. This is for people who care, listen, and want to protect others from the same pain.

A hardworking nurse in Singapore, after long shifts and isolation during COVID, she began chatting with a kind man online. He said he was an interior designer in Canada. They talked daily — sweet messages, photos, even dreams of marriage.

💸 Then he introduced her to "crypto trading". He promised quick profits and offered to "guide" her. She tried it. The website looked real. Her money "grew".

Over time, she: ✅ Invested all her savings ✅ Took a loan ✅ Sent over S$120,000 ($90,000 USD).

😨 Then it all collapsed. The site suddenly demanded "withdrawal fees." When she refused, he vanished — blocked her everywhere.

Everything — the romance, the money, the platform — was fake. 💔

She was left with: No money, huge debt, shame, heartbreak, and depression.

She later filed for bankruptcy and started therapy. Now, she shares her story — not for pity, but to warn others.

📢 Please learn from this:

  • ❌ Don't invest because someone online says "trust me."
  • ❌ Don't send money to someone you've never met.
  • ❌ Don't suffer in silence — talk to someone first, someone you trust and who is non-judgmental.
  • ✅ Do a reverse image search.
  • ✅ Verify every "investment site."
  • ✅ Trust your instincts — not smooth words.

🛑 Scammers use love as a weapon. They don't just steal money — they steal hearts, time, and trust.

If this could happen to a smart, strong nurse — it can happen to anyone. Don't be too arrogant or full of yourself — we are all susceptible in different ways.

🙏 Do well to share this — you might save someone's life or life savings.
